Season in the Sun

Poster Illustrations

A Not-So-Real Summer Music Festival

Season in the Sun is a summer music festival that means to bring relaxation through the music genre, City Pop. City Pop is a music genre that dominated 1980s Japan. It is characterized the use of synthesizers and a catchy beat. The genre of music is believed to have caught on among the youth of the 1980s as a form of escapism from their extremely stressful day to day lives as well as the national tragedies and crimes that shocked and struck fear and anxiety in the Japanese people in general. As such, Season in the Sun hopes to bring at least a few days of happiness and relaxation through escapism.

A series of three 24"x36" posters for a fictitious music festival. Each poster feature a different act that would perform at this fictitious music festival. Each poster features a differnet act that would theoretically be featured at the music festival. Moreover, the posters are designed to mimic the appearance of a typical 1980s Japanese album complete with band that would be wrapped around the album to provide more information and translations that otherwise would be hidden on the back where no listener looked..
